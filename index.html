Leonardo da Vinci

Periods of his life

Portrait of Leonardo da Vinci

Here is the main periods of his biography

  • Early life (1452–1472)

    Leonardo da Vinci was born on 15 April 1452 in, or close to, the Tuscan hill town of Vinci.

    In the mid-1460s, Leonardo's family moved to Florence. Around the age of he became a garzone in the workshop of Andrea del Verrocchio, who was the leading Florentine painter and sculptor of his time. Leonardo became an apprentice by the age of 17 and remained in training for seven years.

  • First Florentine period (1472–1482)

    By 1472, at the age of 20, Leonardo qualified as a master in the Guild of Saint Luke, the guild of artists and doctors of medicine.

    Leonardo's earliest known dated work is a 1473 pen-and-ink drawing of the Arno valley.

    In January 1478, Leonardo received an independent commission to paint an altarpiece for the Chapel of Saint Bernard in the Palazzo Vecchio. In March 1481, he received a commission from the monks of San Donato in Scopeto for The Adoration of the Magi. Neither of these initial commissions were completed.

  • First Milanese period (1482–1499)

    Leonardo worked in Milan from 1482 until 1499.

    He was commissioned to paint the Virgin of the Rocks for the Confraternity of the Immaculate Conception and The Last Supper for the monastery of Santa Maria delle Grazie. In the spring of 1485, Leonardo was commissioned by king Matthias Corvinus to paint a Madonna.

    In 1490 he was called as a consultant, together with Francesco di Giorgio Martini, for the building site of the cathedral of Pavia. Leonardo and his assistants were commissioned by the Duke of Milan to paint the Sala delle Asse in the Sforza Castle.

  • Second Florentine period (1500–1508)

    In 1500 Leonardo fled Milan for Venice. In Venice, Leonardo was employed as a military architect and engineer, devising methods to defend the city from naval attack.

    In Cesena in 1502, Leonardo entered the service of Cesare Borgia, the son of Pope Alexander VI, acting as a military architect and engineer and travelling throughout Italy with his patron.

    Leonardo returned to Florence by early 1503, where he rejoined the Guild of Saint Luke on 18 October of that year. By this same month, Leonardo had begun working on a portrait of Lisa del Giocondo, the model for the Mona Lisa. Then he spent two years in Florence designing and painting a mural of The Battle of Anghiari for the Signoria

  • Second Milanese period (1508–1513)

    By 1508, Leonardo was back in Milan. In 1512, Leonardo was working on plans for an equestrian monument for Gian Giacomo Trivulzio, but this was prevented by an invasion of a confederation of Swiss, Spanish and Venetian forces, which drove the French from Milan. Leonardo stayed in the city.

  • Rome and France (1513–1519)

    From September 1513 to 1516, Leonardo spent much of his time living in the Belvedere Courtyard in the Apostolic Palace. Leonardo was given an allowance of 33 ducats a month, and according to Vasari, decorated a lizard with scales dipped in quicksilver. Leonardo became ill. He practiced botany in the Gardens of Vatican City, and was commissioned to make plans for the pope's proposed draining of the Pontine Marshes. He also dissected cadavers, making notes for a treatise on vocal cords.

    In 1516, Leonardo entered Francis' service, being given the use of the manor house Clos Lucé.

  • Death

    Leonardo died at Clos Lucé on 2 May 1519 at the age of 67, possibly of a stroke.
